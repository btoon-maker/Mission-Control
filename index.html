Orbit Rescue: Mission Control

This summer, you were selected to join the Mission Control internship program, working alongside scientists and satellite tracking specialists connected to Lowell Observatory.

Your internship mentor, Maya Chen, is part of the orbital tracking team that helps monitor research satellites moving around Earth.

As one of the Junior Orbital Response Specialists, your team will help analyze satellite observations, investigate movement patterns, and support ongoing space research.

Maya welcomes your team into Mission Control.

"Our teams study how satellites move through space and help investigate anything unusual we observe," Maya explains.
"Today, we received new tracking data that scientists did not predict."

A research satellite orbiting Earth has started moving differently than expected.

Your team has been asked to investigate the evidence, analyze new observations, and help determine what is happening.

Section 1 of 3
Mission Control Alert

Maya pulls the newest orbital tracking report onto the main screen.

"This is Aurora-3," Maya says. "It's a research satellite designed to study weather patterns and atmospheric changes around Earth."
"Over the last several observation cycles, its movement has started changing in ways we did not predict."

Mission Control systems have reported:

  • unexpected orbital movement
  • timing mismatches in communication signals
  • changes in the satellite's predicted path

Scientists are not yet sure why Aurora-3 is moving differently than expected.

Maya zooms in on the satellite's orbital path.

"Your team is going to help analyze the observation data and figure out what may be causing these changes."
